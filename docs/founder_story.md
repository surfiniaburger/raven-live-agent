# Founder Story and Problem Narrative

I was traveling from Ekiti State to Lagos State after my brother's wedding introduction when we hit a violent storm. In the middle of that storm, a major highway incident unfolded: a large trailer, a nine-seater bus, and several cars were involved in a cascading collision. The bus was trapped under the trailer, another vehicle had gone off the road, and the scene turned chaotic within minutes.

That day made one problem very clear: in severe weather, drivers and first responders often have fragmented information, delayed judgment support, and no live operational assistant to help prioritize safe actions in real time.

RAVEN was built from that pain point.

## Why RAVEN exists
- To help drivers decide early when weather and road conditions no longer support normal speed behavior.
- To help people at a scene prioritize actions that reduce secondary casualties.
- To provide grounded, cited guidance instead of guesswork under stress.

## Product thesis
A live multimodal copilot (voice + camera) can reduce decision lag during high-risk moments by:
1. Detecting contextual risk quickly.
2. Guiding immediate actions in short, safe prompts.
3. Grounding responses in trusted incident and safety knowledge.
4. Producing a handoff-ready incident brief for responders.

## Startup path
- Initial wedge: fleet safety, highway operations, and emergency dispatch support.
- Expansion: insurance FNOL triage, logistics risk operations, and city-level road safety copilots.





In September 2025, my family traveled to Ondo State for my brother's wedding introduction, on our way back there was a huge storm that morning. Not long on the highway getting close to Lagos there was a huge traffic, so we crawled on the road and finally reached the origin of the traffic. A trailer several small cars and a luxury bus which usually contained about 10 people was sandwiched on the middle of the road. Many families were lost that day and this is just a fraction of what happens every year when travelling inter state.

So for this hackathon, I thought deeply of the pain I felt and the pain the families felt to build this project. Using gemini Live API and Agent development Kit we built a first responder app that guides users in tricky situations. The agent fetches data from reserve using Vertex search API. Gemini takes a screenshot of the incident and grounds itself in the data that as been carefully verified and sourced to prepare first responders with details they will need for the rescue.
